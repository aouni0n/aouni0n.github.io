About

I'm a student focused on Computer Engineering, with strong interests in embedded electronics, cybersecurity, and physics — particularly particle and nuclear-level concepts. What keeps me interested is understanding how systems behave at their lowest levels — signals, hardware response, and how software controls physical systems.

Most of my learning happens through building. From rotating radar-style detection systems to microcontroller-driven tools, I prefer testing ideas in hardware rather than leaving them theoretical.

Long-term, I aim to work on advanced computing systems, secure architectures, and physics-informed engineering — the kind of work where reliability, precision, and deep understanding actually matter.
